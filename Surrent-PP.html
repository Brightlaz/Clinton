APARTMENT RENTAL UX CASE STUDY

A quick and reliable way of renting an apartment

Background

Due to Nigeria's substandard mortgage system, an increasing number of people are renting apartments. For one reason or another, people are constantly looking for new flats. These reasons can include family growth, relocation, school, vacation, etc. However, looking for an apartment can be a stressful and time-consuming procedure that drains people's time, resources, and energy.

My Role

I led user research, competitor research, wireframing, user testing, UI design, prototyping

Problem

To aid in the search for a suitable apartment and expedite the procedure, people hire middlemen (agents). With this strategy, an agency fee is required. When an agent accepts many jobs, they fully anticipate not meeting each deadline. Prevarication results from it, which may have an impact on later goals and plans of the tenants.

Tools

Google forms, Figma (Figjam), Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator.

Outcome

I designed a digital platform where potential tenants can search for and rent apartments, and where landlords can post vacancies.

Duration

The project lasted 8 weeks

User Research

User Interview

In order to understand the traditional way of renting an apartment, I interviewed 3 property owners, 5 tenants, and 3 agents that were a reflection of the target audience.

Key Findings

97%

Of respondents confirmed that using the service of an agent increases the cost of rent
Many of them mentioned that they pay in the range of one to four thousand naira daily to agents during the hunt for an apartment, and there is a compulsory agent fee to be paid when the flat is acquired.

80%

Of respondents hunt for an apartment in an average of three months
The delay in acquiring a suitable apartment is caused by different factors. Participant one says that the first thing they consider is an environment with security that is not affected by a flood. People move into an environment without an idea of the environmental condition in which they are forced to live with those conditions since apartment owners rarely refund rent.

60%

Of respondents prefer hunting for apartments themselves
Most people use an agent for apartment hunting due to their busy schedules and unfamiliarity with other territories. They usually end up in an environment they’re not comfortable with since agents don’t usually pay attention to their preferences.

Competitor Research

I researched 4 of Surent's top competitors in the industry. I assessed how the product offerings of these competitors are translated to platform features and studied the apartment rental industry to gain insights into its services, sales, and marketing strategies. I used the information from the analysis as a point of comparison to identify Surent's strengths and weaknesses relative to each competitor. I analyzed the competing companies based on their business model, product offering, unique value proposition, website experience, and the number of areas covered.

Define

User Persona

I created three personas based on user research to gain a perspective similar to the users. The personas helped me better understand user needs and expectations to produce solutions and make design decisions.

Visualizing the product through Story Map

I used a story map to find the parts that matter most and to identify holes in my ideas after coming up with user stories that enfold the needs and goals of the users. I was able to identify opportunities and also established a shared understanding with stakeholders.

Ideate

User flow

I ideated on potential solutions, and user flows based on the user insights obtained from user research, personas, and user story mapping. I created a user flow for a renter to find suitable apartments nearby without creating an account, and also for a property owner.

Information Architecture

The organization, structure, and labeling of content in a sustainable and efficient manner are the main goals of information architecture (IA). Users are supposed to be assisted in finding information and finishing tasks. I designed an information architecture that accounted for the distinct goals that a landlord and a renter have while using the platform.

Design

Drawing out Concepts

Rough drawings are undoubtedly the most easy, iterative, and economical way to begin user interface design. I start by making a list of the information that needs to be on a page and then rapidly sketch down my thoughts since it is quicker and easier to do so. For me, the objective was to express all of my thoughts, thus I remained more concerned with quantity than quality. I was able to quickly draw out my ideas with the help of my issue statements, and I determined on the ones I want to investigate in more detail in a digital wireframe.

Mid-Fi Wireframes

I created mid-fidelity wireframes that were as close to the final designs as I could get using the sketches as a base. I concentrated on interaction design when making the wireframes, making sure the user flow was clear and addressed user issues. It was important to me to design this in sufficient depth so that I could quickly test its basic functions and navigation with users.

Usability Testing - Results and Refinements

Through a moderated usability testing, 10 users—5 renters, 4 landlords, and 1 agent—tested the mid-fidelity wireframes with limited functionality and aesthetics. At this point, the test objectives were to evaluate the effectiveness of the layout and navigation of the landing page, the brand messaging, the onboarding process, the apartment search process, the account creation process, and the apartment upload process.

Before

The ‘search bar’ on the hero section of the landing page was ineffective. 95% of testers wanted to focus their searches so they could quickly locate what they were looking for.

After

To address this, I expanded the search options to include area, property type, and rent range.

Before

TSince listing an apartment and finding an apartment were featured in the same section of the page, 75% of testers were unable to tell them apart.

After

In order to address this, I divided listing and apartment-hunting into separate sections, allowing users to begin their individual journeys.

Before

45% of users expressed dissatisfaction about their inability to add an apartment to view later so they could continue their search.

After

It was necessary for users to rapidly bookmark an apartment they liked before continuing their search since they wanted to choose from a variety of options so I added a ‘save’ icon button to the apartment ads.

UI Design

I used Figma to produce high-fidelity mockups after making the necessary revisions in light of the user testing. I created designs for both desktop and mobile platforms, paying special attention to the mobile experience, since more users would access the website using their mobile phones. The overall design was clean, modern, and uncomplicated. In an effort to improve accessibility, convey states, and create visual contrast, typography, shapes, and colors were used.

Learnings & Outcomes

To begin with, in terms of responsive design, creating a mobile-friendly website was crucial for meeting the needs of users who frequently access rental listings on the go. This included optimizing the website's layout and functionality for smaller screens, as well as making sure that all features were easily accessible and usable on mobile devices. The ability to vet landlords before listing their apartments was an important aspect of the user experience. This entailed implementing a landlord verification process, which helped to ensure that all listings were legitimate and trustworthy. User reviews were also added to the website to provide users with more information about the landlords and their properties.

Finally, a key outcome of the project was the creation of an intuitive user experience. This involved simplifying the search process, making it easy for users to find relevant listings, and providing clear and concise information about each property. Furthermore, the process of submitting an application for an apartment has been streamlined, allowing users to complete the process in a short amount of time.
